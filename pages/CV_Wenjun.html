Research Results

l  Preparation of 2D Materials and Controllable Fabrication of Wrinkled Structures

Preparation of 2D materials, including graphene, MXenes, and Bi2O2Se,by CVD, mechanical exfoliation or other top-down exfoliation methods; fabrication of homogeneous wrinkled structures in graphene with controllable RMS based on Rayleigh-Bénard convection to further tune its mechanical, electrical, optical and interfacial properties; theoretical analysis of relevant mechanisms; transfer of 2D materials onto pre-stretched polymer substrates for precise fabrication of periodic wrinkles.

 

l Study of Physical Properties of Novel 2D Materials Based on Scanning Probe Techniques

Experimental measurement of mechanical properties (Young's modulus, stiffness and flexibility) of 2D Bi2O2Se, a novel two-dimensional semiconductor based on weak electrostatic stacking, for the first time by AFM nanoindentation; revelation of the out-of-plane unipolar resistance switching of Bi2O2Se at nanoscale with CAFM to demonstrate its potential applications in highly-integrated nanoelectronics with new functions; revelation of the resistance switching behaviors of 2D mica to fabricate related RRAM with high performance and achieve non-Markov chain in a single RRAM for the first time.

 

l  Wearable and Flexible Sensors Based on Wrinkled Graphene

Fabrication of flexible mechanical sensors based on wrinkled graphene for detection of tension, torsion, bending and compression. In particular, introduction of a new sandwich-like structure to construct resistive and capacitive pressure sensors based on wrinkled graphene, which not only lowers the energy consumption, but also improves the detection performance; practical applications of these mechanical sensors in motion detection, health monitoring and electronic skins.

For the first time to report a stretchable surface-enhanced Raman scattering (SERS) substrate (based on wrinkled graphene and gold nanoparticles) and its stable detection performance under tension strain; The SERS substrate with the capability to identify multiple analytes simultaneously and tunable identifiability by stretching the substrate; employment of FDTD simulation to demonstrate the operating mechanism.
